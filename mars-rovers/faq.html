Frequently Asked Questions

  1. What is the Curiousity Rover's primary objective?
    2. Curiosity's primary goal is to explore Mars' Gale Crater to learn more about the planet's history and assess its habitability.
  2. How did Curiosity land on Mars?
    3. Curiosity used a "sky-crane" landing system, a unique and complex process, to safely touch down on the Martian surface.
  3. What instruments does Curiosity use to study Mars?
    4. Curiosity carries a suite of scientific instruments, including cameras, spectrometers, and drills to analyze rock samples and detect chemical compositions.
  4. What is Perseveranceâ€™s primary mission on Mars?
    5. Perseverance's main mission is to explore the Jezero Crater, study the Martian geology, and search for signs of ancient life, while also preparing for future human exploration.
  5. What role does the Ingenuity helicopter play with Perseverance?
    6. Ingenuity, a small experimental helicopter, demonstrates powered flight on Mars and assists Perseverance by scouting terrain and identifying interesting locations for study.
  6. How is Perseverance collecting samples for return to Earth?
    7. Perseverance collects rock and soil samples using its robotic arm, sealing them in tubes to be retrieved in a future Mars mission to bring them back to Earth.
